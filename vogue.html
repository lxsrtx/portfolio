Editorial Design + Issue Development

02 | OVERVIEW

Goal

The goal of this project is to create a magazine layout consistent of 34-page that covers profile story, cover story, mini feature and advertisement.

Background

This project took three months to complete. The task involved selecting a magazine brand with a design style I enjoyed and adding my own touch to develop a 34-page magazine on a topic of my choice. The project delved into magazine layout, hierarchy, typography.

03 | STRATEGY

Name of publication

I selected Vogue magazine for its sophisticated and contemporary design, aligning with the aesthetic I aim to achieve in my work. Vogue consistently delivers a refined portrayal of fashion and lifestyle, setting the standard in the industry. Each issue serves as a comprehensive guide, not only showcasing the latest trends but also offering insightful commentary on the cultural nuances influencing the fashion landscape.

Target Audience

Vogue magazine’s target audience are between 20 and 30 years old. They’re usually women living in cities who are into the latest trends and culture. Our readers come from different backgrounds. Some are middle-class, and some have higher incomes, typically between $100,000 and $500,000 a year.

Tone of voice / Message

Vogue’s tone of voice is typically sophisticated, authoritative, and aspirational. It exudes elegance, confidence, and a sense of being at the forefront of the fashion and lifestyle industry. It communicates with a refined and cultured language. The brand message extends beyond the realm of fashion. It emphasizes that fashion is not only about clothing; it encompasses our lifestyles, cultural interests, and sources of inspiration.

04 | Design Process

I think the Vogue magazine project turned out really well. I focused on keeping everything connected across the 40 pages. By using repeated elements, like those solid rectangles, I made sure the design flowed smoothly. I chose a golden yellow color that wasn't too flashy but added a classy touch, keeping things calm and elegant. Using mostly one color made the different colors used for features stand out and kept things interesting. The layout, with lots of white space and some breaks from the usual order, helped keep readers engaged. Overall, the Vogue project is a cohesive and visually appealing piece that I'm proud of.
